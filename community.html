Who belongs in this room

The specific kind of person the Project C community is built for

Project C isn't a general-purpose journalism community or an industry Slack to lurk in. It's a working peer group for journalists who are actively building their own thing — and that shapes who it serves well and who it doesn't. We review every application because the room is only as good as the people in it.

Project C is for you if…

  • You're running a newsletter, podcast, YouTube channel, or other independent media venture — or you've started building one in earnest.
  • You're in the thick of going solo or thinking seriously about it, and you want peers at your level.
  • You want honest, off-the-record conversations about revenue, burnout, growth, and the unglamorous reality of building alone.
  • You'll show up, introduce yourself, and participate — not just lurk.

Project C probably isn't for you if…

  • You work inside a traditional newsroom and are curious what creator journalists are up to.
  • You're a publisher or platform scouting talent to cover or poach.
  • You're a media reporter looking for story leads from inside a private community.
  • You want a passive subscription rather than an actual community to participate in.

The humans running this thing

Who's actually on the other side of the application

Project C was founded by Liz Kelly Nelson — a longtime journalist, editor, and strategist who spent years watching talented reporters leave legacy newsrooms and wonder what the hell to do next. She started Project C to be the thing she wished existed when friends started going independent: a place to trade real numbers, real strategy, and real encouragement without the performative edge of public social media.

Project C is co-moderated by Blair Hickman, a longtime digital strategist and coach who's been in the independent media trenches since before there was a word for it. She also leads the Project C coaching offerings for members who want more hands-on help figuring out their next move.

Between the two of them, Liz and Blair read every application personally. Only one yes is required to get you in — and both of them are pulling for people who are serious about building something of their own.

How applying works

From application to "you're in"

We built this process to be honest and low-friction. Here's exactly what happens after you hit submit.

01

You apply

Fill out the form below, tell us about your work, and pick your membership tier. Takes about five minutes.

02

Card on file, not charged

We vault your payment method through Stripe but don't run it. Think of it like the hold a hotel puts on your card at check-in.

03

We review your application

Every application gets a real read from a human moderator. We aim to respond within three business days — yes, no, or a quick clarifying question.

04

Approved? You're in.

If yes, you're auto-invited to Slack and your card is charged. If no, you hear back gently and your card is never touched.

Coming in through a partner cohort? If you're joining via the Muslim Accelerator, ONA26, or another Project C partner program, enter your cohort code in the form below. You'll skip the card step entirely, and your membership runs through your cohort window.
