Who belongs in this room

Built for a specific kind of journalist

Project C isn't a general-purpose journalism Slack to lurk in. It's a working peer group for journalists actively building their own thing. We read every application, because the room is only as good as the people in it.

It's for you if
  • You're running a newsletter, podcast, YouTube channel, or other independent media venture, or you've started building one in earnest.
  • You're in the thick of going solo, or thinking seriously about it, and you want peers at your level.
  • You want honest, off-the-record conversations about the unglamorous reality of building alone.
  • You'll show up, introduce yourself, and participate. Not just lurk.
Probably not if
  • You work inside a traditional newsroom and are just curious what creator journalists are up to.
  • You're a publisher or platform scouting talent to cover or poach.
  • You're a media reporter looking for story leads from inside a private community.
  • You want a passive subscription rather than an actual community to participate in.

The humans running this thing

Who's on the other side of the application

Project C was founded by Liz Kelly Nelson, a longtime journalist, editor, and strategist who spent years watching talented reporters leave legacy newsrooms and wonder what to do next. She started Project C to be the thing she wished existed when friends went independent: a place to trade real numbers and real encouragement without the performative edge of public social media.

It's co-moderated by Blair Hickman, a longtime digital strategist and coach who's been in the independent media trenches since before there was a word for it. She also leads the Project C coaching offerings for members who want hands-on help with their next move.

Liz and Blair read every application personally. Only one yes is required to get you in, and both of them are pulling for people serious about building something of their own.

Liz Kelly NelsonFounder, Project C

How applying works

From application to "you're in"

We built this to be honest and low-friction. Here's exactly what happens after you hit submit.

01

You apply

Fill out the form below, tell us about your work, and pick your tier. Takes about five minutes.

02

Card on file, not charged

We vault your payment method through Stripe but don't run it, like the hold a hotel puts on your card at check-in.

03

We review it

Every application gets a real read from a human moderator. We aim to respond within three business days.

04

Approved? You're in.

If yes, you're auto-invited to Slack and your card is charged. If no, you hear back gently and your card is never touched.
